Adventure-Quest-Worlds

AdventureQuest Worlds is a browser-based, multiplayer, online MMORPG released by Artix Entertainment in October 2008.

Players are asked to choose from one of four "starting classes" (warrior, rogue, mage, or healer), each with different strengths and weaknesses. Leveling up allows characters to unlock new abilities and increase their attack power. Players have five different skills, depending on their character's class; the ability that is common to all classes is the "auto-attack" ability. Individual abilities, deemed "passive" abilities, may be unlocked after the character reaches a certain Rank. Players begin at Rank 1 and progress as they receive class points from defeating enemies. Players gain experience and gold when enemies have been defeated.

Maple Story

 

MapleStory is a massive multiplayer and free-to-play, 2D MMORPG and was developed in South Korea by a company called Wizet. Although F2P, the game allows for in-game purchases of some character appearances and gameplay enhancements from the Cash Shop.

Players travel the "Maple World", defeating monsters and developing their characters' skills and abilities. Interaction with other players can be through the form of chatting, trading and playing minigames. Groups of players can band together in parties/guilds to hunt monsters and share the rewards. This game follows a sequel with MapleStory 2 being the next in-line: the same concept follows but updated features include 3D graphics and a similar storyline.
